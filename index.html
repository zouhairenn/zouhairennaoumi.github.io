About Me

I am a PhD researcher affiliated with the Department of Economics and Statistics at the University of Salerno. My research sits at the intersection of informal economies, labor markets, inequality, and public policy — exploring how tax evasion, social protection gaps, and informal employment shape economic outcomes.

I have a particular interest in survey-based methods, including survey experiments and discrete choice experiments (DCE), as well as belief formation and policy perceptions. I have received specialized training in DCE design at ISER, University of Essex, and bring both methodological and applied perspectives to my work.

Research Interests

My work spans several interconnected themes in economics and public policy.

🏗️

Informal Economies & Employment

Understanding the structure, drivers, and consequences of economic informality and informal labor markets.

⚖️

Inequality & Social Protection

Examining how income inequality interacts with social protection systems and policy design.

📊

Tax Evasion

Investigating the relationship between tax compliance, informality, and economic development.

🔬

Survey & Choice Experiments

Designing and applying survey experiments and discrete choice experiments to study beliefs, preferences, and policy perceptions.
